You can also fund your studies by taking out a student loan from the government.

Perbadanan Tabung Pendidikan Tinggi Nasional, or PTPTN, is a government body that offers loans for Malaysian students who are pursuing tertiary (after Secondary school) education.

To qualify for these loans, you have to have received offers to study at higher education institutions and the courses you are pursuing are approved and accredited by Malaysian Qualification Agency (MQA) (and most are anyway).

The amount of loan given depends on household income, courses applicants are pursuing and type of education institution (public or private institutions). Applicants may receive loans as low as RM2850 and as high as RM50,000.

Students are free to use the money in any manner they choose to, but be warned, you should pay these loans or risk being blacklisted. As of May 2019, around 356,000 people or equivalent to 16% of indiviudals have never paid their loan instalment once. Before taking out the loan, It is essential that students know there is an interest rate of 1% (or in PTPTN terms, Ujrah), the loan needs to be repaid starting after 12 months of graduating and eligible borrowers need to maintain a GPA of 2.0 during their studies.
